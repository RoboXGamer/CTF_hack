Step 1: Determining the Artifact

The challenge included a clue describing an “early civilization artifact featuring narrative scenes” along with a blurred image.

To investigate this, a reverse image search was conducted using the blurred picture. This was combined with a keyword search such as “early civilization artifact with narrative scenes knife.”

These searches led to several archaeological references that matched the description. The artifact was identified as the Gebel el-Arak Knife, an ancient knife made of ivory and flint that dates to approximately 3300 BCE.

Step 2: Identifying the Museum

The flag format required the museum that currently houses the artifact.

An OSINT search for “Gebel el-Arak Knife current location” indicates that the knife was originally purchased in Cairo in 1914 and is now preserved in the Musée du Louvre in Paris, France.

Step 3: Retrieving the Inventory Number

The final element required for the flag was the artifact’s official inventory identifier within the museum’s collection.
